# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project expects clear, respectful, technically honest collaboration.

That means:

- critique ideas directly, not people
- assume good faith, but be precise about problems
- avoid harassment, insults, dogpiling, and personal attacks
- keep discussions grounded in the code, the behavior, and the tradeoffs

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- personal attacks or abusive language
- discriminatory or harassing conduct
- deliberately disruptive behavior
- bad-faith misrepresentation of another contributor's work or intent
- repeated low-signal spam in issues or pull requests

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close threads, or block contributors whose behavior makes the project harder to maintain or unsafe to participate in.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project spaces including issues, pull requests, review comments, and other public discussions tied to this repository.
